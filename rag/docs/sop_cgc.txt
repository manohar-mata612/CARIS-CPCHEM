STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE — CHARGE GAS COMPRESSOR
SOP Number: SOP-CGC-004
Equipment: CB-CGC-001, CB-CGC-002
Revision: 3.1 | Approved: Plant Manager, Cedar Bayou

=======================================================
1. PURPOSE
=======================================================
This SOP defines the operating, monitoring, and response
procedures for the Charge Gas Compressor (CGC) at Cedar
Bayou ethylene cracker. The CGC is a Safety Critical
Equipment (SCE) item. Deviations from normal operating
parameters must be reported immediately to the shift
supervisor and reliability engineer.

=======================================================
2. NORMAL OPERATING PARAMETERS
=======================================================
Speed:              8,200 - 8,800 RPM
Suction pressure:   0.7 - 0.9 bar
Discharge pressure: 36 - 40 bar
Vibration (RMS):    0.5 - 2.5 mm/s
Bearing temperature: 70 - 90°C
Seal gas pressure:  3.5 - 4.5 bar
Oil pressure:       1.5 - 2.5 bar

=======================================================
3. ALARM RESPONSE PROCEDURES
=======================================================

3.1 VIBRATION HIGH ALARM (RMS > 2.5 mm/s)
  Immediate actions:
    a) Notify shift supervisor within 5 minutes
    b) Notify reliability engineer within 15 minutes
    c) Begin manual vibration log every 30 minutes
    d) Check oil pressure and temperature
    e) Review recent maintenance history in SAP PM
  Do NOT reduce load unless directed by supervisor.

3.2 VIBRATION HIGH-HIGH ALARM (RMS > 5.0 mm/s)
  Immediate actions:
    a) Notify shift supervisor IMMEDIATELY
    b) Initiate controlled load reduction
    c) Prepare to activate standby CGC-002
    d) Contact maintenance for emergency bearing inspection
    e) If RMS exceeds 7.0 mm/s: initiate emergency shutdown

3.3 BEARING TEMPERATURE HIGH ALARM (> 95°C)
  Immediate actions:
    a) Check oil supply pressure and flow
    b) Check cooling water flow to oil cooler
    c) Notify reliability engineer within 10 minutes
    d) If temperature reaches 110°C: shutdown compressor

3.4 KURTOSIS ALARM (> 8.0, if monitored online)
  This indicates bearing impulse fault. Even if RMS is
  within normal range, kurtosis above 8.0 is a serious
  early warning indicator.
    a) Notify reliability engineer immediately
    b) Schedule bearing inspection within 24 hours
    c) Increase vibration monitoring to every 2 hours
    d) Pre-order replacement bearings

=======================================================
4. LOCKOUT TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURE
=======================================================
Before any maintenance on CGC:
  1. Reduce compressor speed to minimum
  2. Close inlet and outlet block valves
  3. Depressurize compressor casing via vent valve
  4. Close steam supply to turbine, apply blind flange
  5. Apply lock to steam turbine trip valve
  6. Verify zero energy state with voltage tester
  7. Apply personal lock and tag — EVERY technician
  8. Verify isolation with operations supervisor
  9. Complete PTW (Permit to Work) form

=======================================================
5. BEARING REPLACEMENT PROCEDURE
=======================================================
Estimated time: 6-10 hours (2 qualified technicians)

Pre-work:
  - Confirm parts available: SKF 6314/C3 (x2 minimum)
  - Confirm oil supply (Mobil DTE Heavy, 20L minimum)
  - Complete LOTO per Section 4
  - Obtain hot work permit if required

Procedure:
  1. Drain bearing housing oil
  2. Remove bearing housing cover
  3. Record pre-removal vibration data and bearing condition
  4. Remove old bearing (use bearing puller, do not hammer)
  5. Clean bearing housing thoroughly
  6. Inspect shaft journal for wear or damage
  7. Install new bearing (use induction heater, 110°C max)
  8. Verify bearing clearance within specification
  9. Reinstall housing cover, torque to 85 Nm
  10. Refill with fresh oil to correct level
  11. Remove LOTO, restart per startup SOP-CGC-001
  12. Take vibration baseline reading within 1 hour of restart
  13. Record all work in SAP PM work order

Post-maintenance verification:
  RMS must be < 1.5 mm/s within 2 hours of restart.
  Kurtosis must be < 3.5 within 2 hours of restart.
  If values exceed these: shut down and reinspect.
